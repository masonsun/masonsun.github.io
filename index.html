About Me

Hi there! I'm Mason. I was born in Richmond, which is known for its rainy weather, occasional media cameos, Asian community, and parking etiquette (disclaimer: no causation). My parents are from Taiwan, but I lived in Vancouver, Taipei, Istanbul, and Eastern Europe, each for four or more years, growing up. I guess home is more of a concept than a physical entity.

I graduated in May 2015 with my bachelor's degrees in computer science and biology from McGill University, where I studied the features of the human-virus interactome under the supervision of Brandon Xia. I have also been involved in research pertaining to microbiology, biomedical engineering, and fluid dynamics at McGill, and cognitive science and functional genomics at the University of Hong Kong and Academia Sinica respectively. I've recently worked at KPMG as a data science intern.

I'm interested in the intersections of media, health, and technology, particuarly using quantitative methods to leverage better decisions and transforming data into actionable insights. I maintain an inventory of things I enjoy reading, sharing, or learning that I call "In the Pursuit of Knowledge." Please consider this knowledge in the loosest of definitions. Feel free to check it out.
