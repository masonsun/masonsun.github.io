About Me

Hi there! I'm Mason. I was born in Richmond, which is known for its rainy weather, occasional media cameos, Asian community, and parking etiquette (disclaimer: no causation, probably). My parents are from Taiwan, but I lived in Vancouver, Taipei, Istanbul, and Eastern Europe, each for four or more years, growing up. I guess home is more of a concept than a physical entity.

I'm interested in the intersections of media, health, and technology, particuarly using quantitative methods to formulate better decisions and transforming data into actionable insights. I have also developed an interest in human-computer interaction recently, mainly to leverage design principles to deliver information in a more accessible and user-friendly manner.

I graduated in May 2015 with my bachelor's degrees in computer science and biology from McGill University.

Experience

I was a data science intern on the Data and Analytics Team. I helped develop a financial analytics platform responsible for customer experience management.

I was a research assistant at McGill University, where I studied the features of the human-virus interactome under the supervision of Brandon Xia. You can read the paper here. I have also been involved in other research efforts related to microbiology, biomedical engineering, and fluid dynamics.

I was a research intern at the University of Hong Kong, conducting computational experiments to determine how humans organize and process information.
